I am currently in my third year of Honours Computer Science at the University of Waterloo. I decided to study computer science after attending a GoEng Girls engineering seminar in grade 8. Since then, I have attended many computer science related seminars and programs and have spoken with professionals to learn more about computer science and related fields of study. Computer science interests me because of its logical nature as well as the challenge and sense of achievement that comes from advanced problem solving. In my spare time, I love solving brain-teasers and puzzles like Planarity and Flow.
Participation in the Shad program at Carleton University in 2011 has been my most rewarding experience to date. Shad brings together the best and brightest high school students from across Canada to develop entrepreneurial, leadership, and teamwork skills. Shad Carleton was a life-changing experience. I had the opportunity to interact with highly gifted peers. We challenged each other, both intellectually and creatively, on an ongoing basis. Our main project was to develop a product to improve the lives of Canadian children with disabilities. Through teamwork and consensus building, my group developed an iPad app to help children with autism communicate. After completing market research, we developed a prototype and a business plan which we then presented to a panel of judges.

Town of Ingersoll

During high school, I completed a successful co-op placement with the Town of Ingersoll's IT Department. I was responsible for creating a website for the Economic Development Department. Shortly after my co-op term ended, the IT Department received funding to hire me to update the Town of Ingersoll website. Both websites were created using the DotNetNuke Content Management System. I was responsible for aesthetics, adding content, and finishing details for the websites. My knowledge of graphic art was combined with critical analysis of other municipal websites to create user-friendly, appealing websites.

Both the Economic Development and the Town of Ingersoll websites have since been updated and my designs are no longer used.
